Unit 9--1024-bit Calculations and Finding Large Prime Numbers

In the next three weeks, I want to cover four things on programming assignments:

A) Doing calculations with 1024 bits.

B) Finding Large Prime Numbers

C) RSA

D) Diffie-Hellman Key Exchange

RSA is fairly simple and the Diffie-Hellman key exchange is very simple. Neither involves writing a loop. Finding large prime numbers involves a fairly simple program. However, we will do all three with arithmetic modulo a large prime number, which makes it non-trivial, but also interesting. Finding a large prime number will probably require a lot of computer time, and you can't really be sure that your program is correct until you have found the prime number, so there is some challenge. To give you some flexibility let's make the results for A) due November 5, B) due November 19, and C) and D) due on the normal schedule for those units. I have written Unit 10 RSA and Unit 11 Key Exchange, and also a note called Finding large Prime Numbers. Your assignment on finding large prime numbers is to find a number P=2s+1 that is prime, and also for which s is prime. There will be a number of suggestions on how to do that in the note on Finding large Prime Numbers.

1) Calculate this table using a loop and getting each value by multiplying the previous value by m. Also calculate m^20*m^25 and also m^25*m^20 and see whether you get m^45 in both cases.

2) Then recalculate the table by using your fast exponentiation function to calculate each value, and again see whether you get the correct values.

3) Pick a number from that table and raise it to the power P-1 . What answer do you expect to get? (phi(p) = p-1) Now pick a number and raise it to the power p-2. Then multiply that result by the original number. This is another way to get the inverse of a number.
